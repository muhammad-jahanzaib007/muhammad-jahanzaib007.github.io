Precision-Recall Curves vs ROC: When to Use Which

Both curves summarise a classifier across thresholds, but they tell very different stories when your classes are imbalanced. Here is how to pick the right one and read it honestly.

Why this choice keeps tripping people up

I have lost count of how many times I have seen a model reported with an ROC AUC of 0.95 on a task with severe class imbalance, presented as evidence of strong performance, only for the precision at any usable recall to be dismal. The two curves are not interchangeable. They are both threshold sweeps over the same confusion matrix, but they weight the classes differently, and that difference matters enormously once the positive class becomes rare. Choosing the wrong one does not just mislead your reader, it can mislead you during model selection, because you end up optimising for a metric that does not reflect the decision you are actually going to make.
